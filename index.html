2. Overview

Focus Nest is a productivity application designed with privacy-first principles:

  • The app does not require user accounts.
  • The app does not operate any backend infrastructure controlled by the developer.
  • Core functionality works offline using local device storage.
  • No user profiles are created by the developer.
  • Focus Nest does not monitor which applications you use. It only detects whether the app is in the foreground during an active focus session to ensure integrity.

3. Data Collection Principles

3.1 Data NOT Collected by the Developer

The developer does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to developer-controlled systems, including: name, email address, phone number, contacts, precise location, photos, or user identifiers linked to identity.

3.2 Data Stored Locally on Device

The app stores the following data only locally on your device:

  • Focus session timers and durations.
  • Session history and statistics.
  • App settings and preferences.

This data remains on the device, is not accessible by the developer, and can be deleted at any time by clearing the app data or uninstalling the application.

4. Third-Party Services

The app uses third-party SDKs that may process data independently:

4.1 Google AdMob (Advertising)

Google AdMob may process Advertising ID (AAID), IP address, and device information. In the EEA, consent is collected via Google UMP (IAB TCF 2.2). Google Privacy Policy.

4.2 RevenueCat (Purchases)

Processes transaction identifiers and subscription status to enable "Elite" features. RevenueCat Privacy Policy.

4.3 Google Play Billing

All payments are handled by Google. The developer never accesses your credit card or payment details.

5. Legal Basis (GDPR)

Data processing is based on:

  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) – for personalized advertising.
  • Contract Necessity (Art. 6(1)(b)) – for processing purchases.
  • Legitimate Interest (Art. 6(1)(f)) – for security and service integrity.

6. International Data Transfers

Third-party providers (Google, RevenueCat) may transfer data to the United States using Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and other legal safeguards approved by the European Commission.

7. User Rights (GDPR / CCPA)

Users have the right to access, delete, or restrict processing of their data. Since the developer does not store your data on servers, these rights are exercised by:

  • Managing permissions in Android system settings.
  • Resetting the Advertising ID in device settings.
  • Deleting local app data.

8. Permissions

The app requests only necessary permissions: Foreground Service (timer operation), Exact Alarms (timing precision), Notifications (session alerts), and Internet Access (billing and ads).

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy occasionally. Continued use of the app constitutes acceptance of the new terms.
